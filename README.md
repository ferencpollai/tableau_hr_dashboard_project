# 👥 HR Analytics Dashboard (Tableau)

## 🧠 Business Context
HR teams often struggle to answer critical questions quickly:

- Are we growing sustainably?
- Where are we losing employees?
- Are there hidden inequalities in pay or performance?

This project was designed to turn raw HR data into **actionable insights** through a structured and interactive dashboard.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective
To build a **comprehensive HR analytics dashboard** that supports both:
- **Strategic decision-making (executive level)**
- **Operational analysis (employee-level detail)**

---

## 🧩 Dashboard Structure

### 🔹 1. Summary View

#### 📊 Overview
- Total hired, active, and terminated employees
- Hiring vs termination trends over time
- Workforce distribution by department and job title
- HQ vs branch comparison (New York as HQ)
- Geographic distribution (state & city)

#### 👥 Demographics
- Gender distribution
- Age group analysis
- Education level breakdown
- Relationship between education and performance

#### 💰 Income Analysis
- Salary comparison by education level and gender
- Salary vs age trends across departments
- Identification of potential pay gaps

---

### 🔹 2. Employee Records View
- Detailed employee-level dataset
- Fully filterable across:
  - Department
  - Role
  - Gender
  - Education
  - Salary
- Designed for deep-dive analysis

---

## 🛠️ Data Engineering Approach

### Synthetic Data Generation
Instead of using static datasets, I created a **realistic HR dataset (8950 records)** using Python.

The dataset includes:
- Employee lifecycle (hire & termination)
- Demographics (age, gender, education)
- Compensation (salary + adjusted salary)
- Performance metrics
- Organizational structure (department, job title)

This approach allowed:
✔️ Full control over data realism  
✔️ Simulation of real-world HR scenarios  
✔️ Better storytelling through data  

---

## 📊 Analytical Focus

This dashboard is designed to answer questions such as:

- Where is employee attrition highest?
- Are certain departments over- or under-staffed?
- Is there a correlation between education and performance?
- Are there salary discrepancies across gender or roles?

---

## 💡 Key Design Principles

- **Clarity over complexity** → executive-friendly layout  
- **Insight over visuals** → every chart answers a question  
- **Dual-level usability** → summary + detailed analysis  
- **Consistency in design** → structured layout & color system
